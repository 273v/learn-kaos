**Goal:** split a document into chunks before you [embed](/how-to/semantic-embeddings) or
[search](/how-to/search-text-with-bm25) it — without cutting sentences in half or losing the
link back to the source. `kaos-nlp-core` offers several strategies; pick the one that
preserves the structure that matters.

```bash
uv run examples/chunk-a-document.py
```

**Notes**

- `SentenceChunker(max_tokens=...)` packs whole sentences up to a token budget (never
  splitting mid-sentence); `SectionChunker` splits on heading structure. There are also
  fixed-token, paragraph, and hierarchical chunkers.
- Every `Chunk` carries a `char_span` into the source text — so a retrieved chunk traces
  back to exactly where it came from, the basis for [grounded](/concepts/grounding-and-verification)
  results.
- `overlap_sentences=N` adds sentence overlap between chunks when you want context to bleed
  across boundaries.
- Chunking is the step *before* embeddings and retrieval — see how they compose in the
  [research agent](/tutorials/research-agent-citations).
